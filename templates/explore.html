Explore: Climate Change and Deforestation

The term 'climate change' refers to how through human activities (predominately burning fossil fuels) the earth's concentration of greenhouse gases has increased, resulting in the warming of the planet. Carbon dioxide is the waste product of burning fossil fuels; increased global industrialisation in recent years has caused the rapid global acceleration of climate change by increasing carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.

It's not all over yet though. Trees play a big role in reducing greenhouse gases as they take in the excess carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and release oxygen. Through this, trees provide a way of reducing the harmful affects of air pollution produced by human activities. As a result of this, deforestation reduces the collective impact that trees have on the environment and climate change as a whole.
