The UK has pledged to to be carbon neutral by 2050


Why plant trees?

Trees absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen, creating cleaner air and helping combat climate change. They play a significant role in keeping the environment healthy by reducing pollution. Planting trees play an important role in achieving the goal of net-zero greenhouse-gas emissions by 2050 in the UK.

Is climate change real?

Scientific evidence for climate change is unequivocal. Ranging from the planets average surface temperature rising around 1.18 degrees Celsius since the late 19th century, to the global sea level rising around 8 inches in the last century; a change needs to occur. Climate change is showing no signs of stopping,we need to make a stand.

How bad is deforestation?

Forests cover roughly around 30 percent of the world's land area, but they are being destroyed at an alarming rate. Nearly a billion acres of forest have been destroyed over the past 30 years, with 17% of the Amazonian rainforest being destroyed over the past 50 years. Deforestation affects both people and animals with eighty percent of the planets land animals and plants living within forests, threatening many species.
